The World's Major Large Cats

Lion (Panthera leo)

The lion is a powerful feline, often called the "King of the Jungle." They live in prides and dominate the African savannas.

Tiger (Panthera tigris)

Tigers are the largest big cats, known for their striking orange coats with black stripes. They are solitary hunters.

Jaguar (Panthera onca)

Jaguars have the strongest bite of any big cat and thrive in rainforests and wetlands of the Americas.

Leopard (Panthera pardus)

Leopards are agile climbers and adaptable predators, found across Africa and Asia.

Snow Leopard (Panthera uncia)

Snow leopards are elusive mountain-dwelling cats with thick fur for cold climates.

Puma (Puma concolor)

Pumas, also called mountain lions, are versatile predators found across the Americas.

Cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus)

Cheetahs are the fastest land animals, adapted for speed with their slender build and long legs.
